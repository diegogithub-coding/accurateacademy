Explanation:

Possessive adjectives are words that show ownership or possession. They are used to indicate that something belongs to someone. In English, these adjectives tell us who owns or possesses the noun they are describing.

Table:

Possessive Adjective Singular Plural
First Person my our
Second Person your your
Third Person his/her/its their

Structure:

Possessive adjectives are typically placed before a noun to show possession. The structure is: Possessive Adjective + Noun

Examples:

  • English: This is my book. (Este es mi libro.)
  • English: Is this your pen? (¿Es este tu bolígrafo?)
  • English: His cat is very playful. (Su gato es muy juguetón.)
  • English: She always brings her laptop to class. (Ella siempre trae su laptop a clase.)
  • English: The students are doing their homework. (Los estudiantes están haciendo su tarea.)
